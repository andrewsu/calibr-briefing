Lilly's injectable Zepbound already hits both GLP-1 and GIP receptors, and that dual mechanism is a big part of why it's been taking market share. The thinking is that engaging multiple metabolic pathways produces greater weight loss, better metabolic outcomes, and potentially disease-modifying effects on conditions like MASH and cardiovascular disease.

And this is exactly where Calibr's CMZ371 sits. CMZ371 is an oral GLP-1R/GIPR bispecific incretin in Phase 1. Let me emphasize that: it's oral AND dual-targeting. Right now, Lilly has the only approved GIP/GLP-1 dual agonist, but it's injectable. Novo's oral offerings are GLP-1 only. An oral dual agonist would essentially combine the best of both worlds — the convenience of a pill with the superior efficacy of multi-receptor engagement.

Calibr also has obesity biologics in the discovery stage — dual and triple agonists, oral peptide and long-acting injectable formats. So the institute is essentially building a portfolio strategy that spans the full spectrum of next-generation metabolic therapeutics.
