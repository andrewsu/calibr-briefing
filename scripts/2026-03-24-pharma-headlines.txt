Welcome to the Calibr-Skaggs Daily Briefing for Monday, March 24th, 2026. I'm Nigel, and here's your roundup of the day's top biotech and pharma headlines.

The biggest deal of the day: Gilead is acquiring Ouro Medicines for $2.2 billion in a front-loaded transaction that brings in an autoimmune T-cell engager platform. Gilead is paying $1.68 billion upfront, and the deal could also breathe new life into its long-stalled partnership with Galapagos. T-cell engagers for autoimmune disease are having a moment — just yesterday, Sanofi signed a separate $1.2 billion deal with Kali Therapeutics for a phase 1-stage trispecific T-cell engager antibody, marking Sanofi's return to the TCE space.

In the obesity space, a cautionary tale: Aardvark Therapeutics has halted trials of its obesity candidate, putting its entire pipeline on pause. This comes less than a month after Aardvark paused a phase 3 trial for a related asset due to cardiac safety concerns. Worth watching — cardiac signals in obesity drugs remain a critical issue for the field.

On the positive side, Apogee Therapeutics published phase 2 eczema data that analysts are calling a genuine threat to Lilly, Regeneron, and Sanofi. Their anti-IL-13 antibody showed strong enough results that peak sales forecasts have been doubled to $5.2 billion.

In deal news, Merck is paying Flagship Pioneering's Quotient Therapeutics $20 million upfront for access to its somatic genomics platform for IBD drug target discovery. And Shionogi is paying Apnimed $100 million to take full control of their sleep disorder joint venture.

A few more quick hits: Ocugen's eye disease gene therapy hit its phase 2 endpoints with significant lesion reduction at 12 months, though a smaller effect size compared to earlier preliminary data sent the stock down slightly. Innovent Biologics showed its anti-VEGF eye disease drug matched Eylea in a Chinese phase 3 study, setting up local approval filings. And GE HealthCare secured FDA clearance for its Photonova Spectra photon-counting CT scanner, claiming it captures up to 50 times more data than previous premium CT systems.

On the pharma landscape front, Novartis joined Lilly and AstraZeneca in expanding its China presence, committing roughly $480 million for manufacturing and R&D facility upgrades in Beijing and Shanghai.

And in regulatory news, the FDA has scheduled a public hearing for June 12th to gather feedback on the controversial National Priority Voucher review program, which has so far resulted in four ultra-fast drug reviews.
